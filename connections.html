Characters & Tensions

Okonkwo — defender of custom

Okonkwo represents strict tradition. He believes the old ways must never change. His fear of weakness makes him reject anything new, even when it hurts his family and himself.

Nwoye — pulled by a new story

Nwoye feels trapped by harsh parts of tradition and by his father’s expectations. He is drawn to the missionaries because their message feels kinder and more comforting to him.

Obierika — the reflective witness

Obierika is loyal to his culture but also sees its problems. He explains how the clan begins to fall apart when new beliefs divide the people.

Missionaries & colonial rule

They introduce new religion, laws, and ways of thinking. At first many villagers ignore them, but soon they gain followers and split the community.

Key Moments

1) Okonkwo breaks the Week of Peace

He uses violence during a sacred, peaceful time. This shows how he places his own beliefs above the community’s rules and how tradition can be broken from within.

2) Missionaries gain converts

They come quietly and gain trust from some villagers. Their presence challenges the clan’s religious, social, and political traditions. This brings major change.

Symbols & Language

Proverbs

Proverbs are a key part of Igbo tradition. They show shared wisdom and values. When new beliefs enter the village, this shared language starts to fade.

Yams

Yams symbolize tradition, masculinity, and success. They show how the clan values hard work and stability. As change comes, these values feel threatened.

Locusts

Locusts stand for the arrival of something that seems harmless at first but becomes destructive—like the coming of the colonizers.
