Textual Evidence

Short, accurate lines that show Tradition vs. Change. Each quote has a one‑sentence explanation of what it means in the story.

“He has put a knife on the things that held us together and we have fallen apart.”

— Obierika, Ch. 20

The clan’s unity is broken by new power and new belief.

“The white man is very clever.”

— Obierika, Ch. 20

Missionaries arrive quietly and win followers, changing the clan from the inside.

“Okonkwo ruled his household with a heavy hand.”

— Narrator, Ch. 2

Okonkwo believes strength means control; he resists any change in his home.

“his whole life was dominated by fear, the fear of failure and of weakness.”

— Narrator, Ch. 2

He fears becoming “weak,” so he refuses to bend even when he should.

“Proverbs are the palm‑oil with which words are eaten.”

— Narrator, Ch. 1

Proverbs show how important language and tradition are to the Igbo people.

“Age was respected… but achievement was revered.”

— Narrator, Ch. 1

The clan values both elders and hard work; outside change disrupts this balance.
